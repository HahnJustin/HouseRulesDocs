# The Review - I hated it

Gambonanza IS what you get if you want to literally make a chess balatro in marketing with no substance behind it.

![[Pasted image 20260318171459.png]]

Never have I played a game recently that pissed me off this much. Reason being it markets incredibly effectively but will let down players if the final game plays at all like the demo that was released last month. It is currently planning to release in May, but I believe the design problems are so central to this game that it is almost beyond repair.

The core mechanics are you get to place pieces on a smaller 5x5 chess board, but you only get three pieces and when a piece gets taken, its taken for good. This is terrible, you do not get to build a board state and have iteration via pieces, since they are expendable. This is the exact opposite approach of Balatro, which builds a deck as well. This design choice makes your pieces feel pointless. Not only that but all the 'gambits' or equivalents of 'jokers' are based on the type of piece, which doesnt matter since your pieces get taken all the time. You think, well if you play better then they won't get taken, right? No, because you cannot trade pieces as they die forever and the stage starts falling apart if you take too long. Think too much? Oh now pieces just randomly start dying. The game also packs in all these annoying references to gambling, pachinko, capsule balls, etc. But they just make the flow of the game slow down and you get stuck in these stupid menus longer then you actually play the chess. Not to mention that the AI is brain dead and the game looks like shit and theres all this random Japanese shit stuffed in the game too. 

I feel so personally attacked by someone mimicking what we want to do so closely but also making such a bad game while marketing it excellently. Mark my words this game will make around half a million at least will mildly positive reviews and no memorability. It doesn't close the door for making the 'chess balatro' but it sure doesn't make it easier, consumers will be wary since they just bought something that sucked and promised the same thing.

Even though itd be worse for us it was a good game, some how having it be bad but share so much with our idea, makes me feel bad too.

Freaking Gambonanza. Its a derivative cash grab speeding into the lane in front of us.

Ignore it when it launches in May 2026, play shotgun king instead

## The Marketing

Why do I think the marketing is effective? It mimics Balatro so effectively that its riding its coat tails into a fortune. The creator has already said he has 50,000 wishlists as well as the fact that he has a good publishing company, Sidekick Publishing. They made [Die in the Dungeon](https://gamalytic.com/game/2026820), which grossed about a million dollars. Just off the fact the trailer also has 100k views plus the sway of the publishing company, plus the polished store page, plus the wishlists, this game will earn money. Its disadvantage is that all the people drawn in by the Balatro messaging will be dissapointed, like myself. It is not a good game. But people will buy it. So in a way we should learn from this game, you can make a shit ton of money doing barely anything original and focusing just on selling more then actually making anything.
