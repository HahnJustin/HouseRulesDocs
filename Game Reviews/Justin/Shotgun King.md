### Description
Shotgun King is a roguelike chess game where you play as the black king with a shotgun and play against a full board of chess pieces. Each stage you choose an ability to buff yourself which is paired with an ability that buffs the enemy. Fight to stage twelve to win the base game.

### Developer Background
The developers are a team called *Punkcake Delicieux*. They're a French team of two developers and a musician. They focus on making polished titles quick, having an approach to game development that is very similar to that of a content creator. They also have a sizeable following [on patreon](https://www.patreon.com/punkcake).

![[Pasted image 20260315152730.png]]

They also have made several other games on itch prior to release of Shotgun King. They seem to have gotten significant prior attention, as viewable by the patreon chart above as well. Having around 50+ patrons before the release of Shotgun King onto Steam. 

### Background
The game was made in April 2022 actually for a Ludum Dare Jam #50. The game won 1st overall, 1st in fun, and 2nd in innovation. Sound familiar? They released the game [a month later](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/1972440/view/3177863179090003412?l=english) on to steam. The game has been ported to many different platforms.

### Profit
The game seems to have sold around 500,000 units according to [this steam post](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/1972440/view/502827075746398770?l=english). The steam numbers indicate around 275,000 copies sold, which likely means almost half of its sales are from other platforms, such as switch or PlayStation. Regardless this game seems to have made likely around 4-5 million dollars in total. In addition at least the steam release is self published, meaning  the around $2 million in steam profit goes straight to the developers.

# The Review 

## The Demo
This is just from my experience so far as I've played the demo. I think the developers and the way in which this game  became popular as well as the content of the game are so similar to our own situation that I should dig deeper into this game significantly so. So I will do that later, but these are my thoughts from the demo exclusively as of now.

### What the game does well mechanically?
The game is unique, where you play as the black king only and there are no other pieces on your side of the chess board. The king, as he is equipped with a shotgun, plays almost like a turn-based tactical game like XCOM rather than a chess game. But as the opponent plays close to or similar to a normal chess player, there's an interesting asymmetry in how you fight versus how you are attacked. 

In addition after each stage, you get to choose between two options to make the game more difficult and to make you more powerful. Its a really quick onboarding process.

### What could it improve on? / What did I not like

* Chess Pieces can move simultaneously, it makes the game not feel like chess at all I find it incredibly unintuitive
* It looks like the demo is an earlier version of the game that is more difficult than the full release.
* The soul mechanic is un-intuitive and not explained well in the demo.

Overall I think they should remake the demo with some of the features in the new game and the remove some of that new content in order to make the game more appealing.

### What the game does well in marketing?
The absurdity of combining chess with a shotgun, I think sells interest by itself. That will be a hard thing to copy. 

In addition they have several youtube trailers as well as a fanbase that definitely gives their games the initial boost they need to get traction on steam. 

### After playing am I surprised by how much it made?

After playing the demo. Yes I am very surprised. The game was made in a shorter jam and has less content and is less enjoyable than our game in my opinion. I think the concept of chess plus gun has been very attractive to players and I think especially was easily leveraged by content creators online. 

This game has really made me think that you can charge $6/10 for a month of development and ride a simple hook into success then slow roll updates and multiple platform releases until becoming a multi million dollar title. 

Which is very interesting and I think worth thinking about.
