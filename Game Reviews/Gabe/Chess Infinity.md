# The Review 

## The Demo

My first impressions of the game are that it feels very much like an Unreal Engine game. It just has that robotic and artificial gloss over it. I thought the game was ok; I felt a little underwhelmed with how it was packaged. Gameplay itself felt like Michael Bay chess directing chess (in a bad way).
### What the game does well mechanically?

It sports this coaching / chess AI that is supposed to help you, which I believe is togglable. It's not terrible, especially in a game like this where it is really just a "regular chess game." It shows when you have certain pieces in harm's way and an option for it to decide your next best strategic move. It does remove some of the thinking you have to do, but it isn't a terrible feature, I'd imagine, for people just learning how to play the game.
### What could it improve on? / What did I not like

To be honest this game really felt like a worse version of table top simulator.

1) Aesthetics & Packaging
The game and its environments are pretty and have nice shading, but the atmosphere doesn't contribute to the gameplay that much. The game has a ton of AI slop art throughout it, which doesn't help that artificial gloss I mentioned. From the AI characters you face to different profile emblems and such, it just makes the game feel somehow more unfinished. I'm not a big fan of the meta UI as well; it just seems weird for this type of game.

2) Sound & Music 
Out of place selection of music and sounds. The music is very reminiscent of being stuck in a sort of limbo. It's half dramatic and takes itself a little too seriously for this type of game. I feel like a more calming, sultry music would have done it a lot better. Sounds I felt weren't appropriate for this game too. There are NO chess placing sounds, which I find weird. Placing pieces has a very strange sensation, and in tandem with the sounds feels empty. The game has a lot of dramatic effects that go on during its gameplay, like explosions, screen rumbling, and kill effects. It all seems a lot with the different sounds.
### What the game does well in marketing?

he trailer was made pretty well, and they have clean shots of the gameplay. It shows most of what's included, so you know what you're getting yourself into.

### After playing am I surprised by how much it made?

Honestly, not at all. I couldn't see this game making a ton of money, with all due respect. I feel like I ragged on this game a lot, but what it added didn't seem to make it more fun. It just wasn't something that stood out to me and surprised me when I played it.
