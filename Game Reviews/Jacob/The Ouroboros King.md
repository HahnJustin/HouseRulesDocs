### Description
*The Ouroboros King combines the strategic depth of chess with the build variety and replayability of roguelikes. Assemble a formidable army, discover powerful relics, and buy surprising gadgets to defeat the Coven.*

### Developer Background
He was a data scientist for 8 years, and interested in game development. He had developed some game jam games, before spending time on a side project, *The Ouroboros King*. This game ended up being more successful than he anticipated (netting him about $87k), which convinced him to quit his job and go into game dev full time. He would release a second game to less success, and in continuing with game development. 

### Background
Game was released 2023, which was met with enough interest to eventually make it on the steam Daily Deals page, and used discounts and other sites to continue driving sales and wishlist conversions. 

A Unity game.

### Profit
Really focused on language support. Ended up porting to Mac / Linux / iOS / Andriod later, some of which were profitable. 

# The Review 
I think I, [[Jacob]], will end up returning this game. It's a roguelike chess, where you build your board with pieces, you gain some abilities, and you can purchase consumables. The core of the game is good, and I think that is why it has done somewhat well, but something about it doesn't grab you. The feel isn't there. The images are static as well as the text, the maps are basic, and the game grows quickly before you understand what is going on. 

I think it suffers the pitfalls of chess-like games, where it makes the already complicated game of chess even more complicated. 

For a bit more of a review, the game there are 4 different enhancements you can navigate to. Add more pieces (up to 7), upgrade the pieces you have (but only some pieces?), buy consumables for the future, buy persisting enhancements. You pick paths to the "boss", but you kind of get stuck into these paths through each "world". 

Some pieces are:
- chess piece combinations
- move only 2 or more
- move like a king or anywhere around the perimeter
- Capturing piece also dies
- Keeps king alive if nearby
- shield piece

Board configurations:
- portals
- Boulders 

Some consumables:
- undo
- any piece moves like a rook
- prevent enemy piece from moving
- break a boulder

Some rituals:
- prevent enemy from using portals
- place one boulder at the start of the round
- when a piece makes the first capture, it is upgraded from the remainder of the game

How they handle draws: advance, but don't get any upgrades, "Bosses" can't draw though. 

### What the game does well mechanically?
There are some interesting pieces and abilities that add interest. 

### What could it improve on? / What did I not like
New pieces / boards / abilities just appear, sometimes for the enemy, without really being introduced? It isn't intuitive what they do, and it very quickly doesn't feel like chess. 

Winning also feels often like using an OP ability and just winning instead of a battle. Like make any piece move like a rook and just take the king. Or get a catapult and lock the king in a spot and just destroy him. 

The AI also doesn't feel great, it's definitely a greedy search with a basic, no-loop condition. I can tell when it repeats, make a different move, then goes back to repeating, and the cycle continues. 

The game rapidly gets too complicated and evolves really quickly. 

### What the game does well in marketing?
Interesting to see his own [reflection](https://oriolcosp.com/the-ouroboros-king-1-year-post-mortem/) on the game launch with an analysis of what paid marketing was beneficial. He did partner with a publisher, but they seem Chinese focused, which may not have helped his game so much. I don't think it did marketing wisely.

### After playing am I surprised by how much it made (or didn't)?
I'm a little surprised it made as much as it did. I feel like the game is not fully built, it feels very mechanical, and it still somehow was nicely profitable for a solo dev as a side project.

### How does it relate to possible implementations of our game?
I think this game is a good game to keep in mind for things to not do while we are developing. It has very similar ideas to us, with new piece types with the foundation of chess, but it doesn't necessarily play great. It's also a game that *on-paper* sounds really interesting, but the final game isn't super interesting.
