### Description
*Face off against the bridge troll in his bizarre game of chess! Decrypt, Twist and Enforce the rules as your devious opponent attempts to break them in King of the Bridge!*

### Developer Background
Made by two Dutch students, Finn Schuuring (sole dev.) and Hogeschool voor de Kunsten Utrecht, and won the [Dutch Game Awards](https://dutchgamesassociation.nl/2024/10/03/and-the-winners-of-the-dutch-game-awards-2024-are/) in 2024. They didn't seem to have done any press, they haven't released any other games, I can't find anything else about them, just made a fun, small game a called it a day. 

Interesting [interview](https://denagames.nl/digitaal/interview-met-indieontwikkelaar-finn-schuuring/), and he is working on a part two in the chess world...

### Background
The game launched in March 2024, won an award in October of that year for best student game, mainly developed by one developer and a friend doing sound and visuals. 

### Profit
The game seems to be estimated to have sold around 65,000 copies, likely making around $100,000 for the small team. It seems to have only been released on steam, with no additional PR or resources put in to the launch. 

# The Review 

I, [[Jacob]], really like game, will likely not return it, but I would have some feedback. The premise of the game is simple, it is twist on chess where your opponent (and you sometimes) can cheat and there are lots of additional rules. These rules are slowly revealed to you as you play, and there is a small narrative in the game which makes things interesting. *Feel* is very good and it feels like a polished game. Audio is crisp, but mainly one loop and some sound effects. 

I beat the game in about an hour. Now there are alternative endings and more rules that I could discover, which I'm honestly interested in doing, but aside from some other narratives, there isn't any more game. This size of the game likely is the reason it is priced as it is. 

The game is a little difficult to start playing, I put it down after 30 minutes of fighting with it and losing for unknown reasons, and if I didn't have to play it more I may have called it quits there. 

### What the game does well mechanically?

The game makes the rules sound easy and fun. The player has a book showing legal moves, which defines the win conditions, and describes what is going on. All of which feels very nice. 

The AI feels good. It isn't too strong, but it punishes you for mistakes. I think if you play enough you can find the weaknesses, but the ability for the AI to cheat and not lose is a real equalizer, which keeps things close. 

The game has a consistent art aesthetic that is really nice and a flowy-but-pixel feel that is very good.  

Definitely worth noting, the Steam achievement system is a really nice touch, feel very polished, makes it feel like there's lots to explore (even in a small game), fun to see pop up and compare with friends. 

### What could it improve on? / What did I not like

* Difficult to get started, the rules haven't been revealed, and can even contradict the ones you know, so you lose early at the start and it is a little frustrating. 
* There is an open world-ness to it (you can wander around a seemingly endless bridge), and while there are some things you find, it seems like you can only go back and battle the troll, which isn't really clear
* It's tough that a game built around "cheating", really means only you get to cheat if the opponent cheats.

Overall, I think people who like puzzles / chess games will power through, and once you've learned enough (both the literal rule book as well as about the game) it becomes and enjoyable, well made small game. 

### What the game does well in marketing?

It seems like they reached out to a bunch of reviewers and convinced them to publish review on or around launch day, but given its ranking on steam pages now, in both chess and the broader board game category, I think both of those did s strong job keeping it in front of people. I think for little marketing spend, this was very successful. 

### After playing am I surprised by how much it made?

At first I was surprised by the price point given how positive the reviews are, so I thought they were leaving money on the table, but in reality I think they priced it according to the amount of gameplay they have, and I think they nailed that. I think for a student doing this on the side (he is studying under a game developer to be a game developer), I think this is a great success and given the positivity around this game, I think it will easily bootstrap his next game.
